Explore careers

Science+Maths

After PCM combination, you can pursue courses in Engineering (Btech/B.E.), Architecture (B Arch), Computer Science (BCA, B.Sc. IT), Pure Sciences (B.Sc. in physics, chemistry, Maths), or allied fields like BioTechnoloogy, Pharmacy(B Pharm) and even Medical (BAMS, BHMS) if biology was also an subject .

Science+Biology

You can pursue medical degrees like MBBS, BDS, BAMS, BHMS, and allied health sciences such as B.Sc. Nursing and B.V.Sc (Veterinary Science). Alternatively, explore non-medical options like B.Sc. in Biotechnology, Microbiology, Forensic Science, Food Technology, Pharmacy (B.Pharm), or Agriculture. Many other specialized fields, such as Paramedical courses and even fields like Nutrition & Dietetics or Environmental Science, are also available

Commerce

You can pursue traditional degrees like Bachelor of Commerce (B.Com), professional courses like Chartered Accountancy (CA) and Company Secretary (CS), management courses such as Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA), or specialized degrees in fields like Economics, Finance, Accounting, and Law (LLB).

Complete roadmaps (step-by-step)

Year 1 (Now — next 12 months)

Focus on strengthening Physics, Chemistry & Maths. Target JEE Main/State CET. Daily: 2–3 hours concept study + 1 hour problem solving.

Year 2

Join a coaching/online course if needed, increase practice tests (mock exams). Start simple coding (Python) and small projects.

After Admission

Participate in clubs, internships, open-source. Build a portfolio of projects & start placement prep from 2nd year.

Year 1

Focus on NEET subjects (Biology, Physics, Chemistry). Strict revision schedule, NCERT first.

Year 2

Full-length mocks, time management practice, focus on weaker topics. Apply for state counselling after results.

After Admission

Clinical exposure, internships, and consider postgraduate study or government medical service exams later.

Immediate (12th passed)

If you plan for SSC or Banking (PO/Clerk), start basics of English, Quant, Reasoning. Many posts accept 12th/graduate — check exam eligibility.

Medium term (after graduation)

For UPSC: most aspirants start serious preparation after graduation. Build reading habit, current affairs, and optional subject foundations.

Long term

Gain work experience, choose specialized government exams (railways, state PSCs, defense) as suitable. Consider coaching & mentorship.
